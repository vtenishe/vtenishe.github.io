CLPS CP-11

Mission Overview

Launch: 2026
Duration: 2 weeks
Lead: Intuitive Machines (IM) with NASA as a partner
Operations: NASA Commercial Lunar Payload Services (CLPS)
Status: Flight mission in the final stages of lander integration

MSFC's Role

Dr. Haviland is the lead CLPS Project Scientist responsible for integrating payload operations across the NASA suite of science and technology payloads.

Mission Description

Intuitive Machines' Nova-C lander will deliver four NASA CLPS science payload suites to the Reiner Gamma lunar swirl on the lunar nearside. These include:

  • Lunar Vertex (PRISM1a), led by the Applied Physics Laboratory: a joint lander-rover investigation, magnetometers on both, an ion-electron spectrometer, cameras and a multispectral microscope on the rover.
  • Cooperative Autonomous Distributed Robotic Explorers (CADRE) investigation (led by JPL) consisting of three suitcase sized rovers carrying a Ground Penetrating Radar (GPR).
  • European Space Agency's MoonLIGHT Pointing Actuator (MPAc), a next generation retroreflector.
  • Korea Astronomy and Space Science Institute (KASI) Lunar Space Environment Monitor (LUSEM): a pair of solid state telescopes detecting high energy particles.

Scientific Objectives

Key measurements include:

  1. The origin of lunar magnetic fields and their relation to surface swirls features
  2. Testing autonomous roving capabilities
  3. Measuring the size, shape, and liquid core of the Moon. Also, a target to measure Earth-Moon distance

Scientists know that lunar swirls exist, but we do not know exactly what they are or how they form. We do know they are related to the Moon's magnetic field. Lunar Vertex will take on some persistent lunar mysteries: the origins of these magnetic anomalies and any relation they might have to the visible swirls. The investigation will look closely at how these areas interact with the solar wind, the high-speed stream of energetic particles from the Sun.
