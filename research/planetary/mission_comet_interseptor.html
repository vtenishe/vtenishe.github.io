Comet Interceptor

Mission Overview

Launch: 2029 (planned)
Target: Dynamically new comet
Duration: TBD based on target selection
Partners: ESA (lead), JAXA, NASA
Status: Development Phase

MSFC's Role

ST 13 is contributing expertise in several key areas:

  • Modeling of dusty-gas coma
  • Data analysis and interpretation of the observations

Mission Description

Comet Interceptor is a first-of-its-kind mission to visit a truly pristine comet or other interstellar object that is only just starting its journey into the inner Solar System. The mission will travel to an as-yet undiscovered comet, making a flyby of the chosen target when it is on the approach to Earth's orbit.

Unlike previous comet missions, which visited short-period comets that have passed through the inner Solar System many times, Comet Interceptor aims to target a comet visiting the inner Solar System for the first time â€“ perhaps one from the vast Oort cloud that surrounds the outer reaches of the Sun's realm.

Scientific Objectives

The mission aims to characterize a dynamically new comet or interstellar object, including:

  • Surface composition and morphology
  • Chemical composition of the coma
  • Structure of the plasma environment
  • Properties of dust particles and gas emissions
  • Subsurface characteristics through remote sensing

By studying a pristine comet that has never been altered by the Sun's heat, scientists hope to understand the original material that formed our solar system over 4.5 billion years ago.
