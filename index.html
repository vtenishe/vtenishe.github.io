Research Areas

Research conducted at ST 13 includes important heliophysics and planetary science investigations and missions, and spans from surface of the planetary satellites to the plasma in the solar system, to the Sun dynamics and that of space radiation in the heliosphere and geospace.

Dusty Plasma Laboratory (DPL)

Lunar Science

Investigating the Moon's exosphere, plasma environment, and surface interactions with solar radiation, supporting Artemis and future lunar exploration.

Planetary Satellites

Studying the atmospheres and surfaces of icy moons like Europa and Enceladus, with focus on their potential subsurface oceans and interaction with their planetary environments.

Solar-Planetary Interactions

Investigating how solar energetic particles (SEPs) and cosmic rays affect planetary environments, with applications to space weather prediction and radiation protection for astronauts.

Missions and Instrumentation

Developing cutting-edge instruments and contributing to space missions that advance our understanding of the solar system, from the Sun to planetary bodies and their moons.
