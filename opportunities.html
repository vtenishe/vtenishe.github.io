Research and Career Opportunities

The Heliophysics and Planetary Science Branch (ST13) at NASA's Marshall Space Flight Center offers various opportunities for students, postdoctoral researchers, and scientists to engage in cutting-edge space science research.

Student Opportunities

We offer several pathways for students to gain hands-on experience in heliophysics and planetary science research:

  • NASA Internships - Undergraduate and graduate students can apply for summer, fall, and spring internships working on active research projects. Apply through NASA Internships
  • Graduate students - NASA supports graduate students through the multiple programs and opportunities. In addition, it may be possible to arrange a visit to collaborate with us at MSFC on joint research. Contact one of us for more information.
  • Undergraduate students - We offer summer projects for undergraduates interested in planetary science. Our students often present at conferences and may experience launches or mission activities. Apply through the NASA undergraduate opportunities and contact us directly about your interest. Advanced undergraduates can also apply for the NASA Academy summer program.
  • NASA STEM Engagement Programs - Including the Space Grant program which provides funding for student research projects. Learn about NASA STEM programs
  • Pathways Program - Federal internships and employment opportunities for students and recent graduates. Pathways Program details

Postdoctoral Fellowships

Early-career scientists can join our research teams through several postdoctoral programs:

  • NASA Postdoctoral Program (NPP) - Administered by Universities Space Research Association (USRA), this program offers competitive stipends and research allowances. Annual application deadlines are March 1, July 1, and November 1. Candidates must apply through the NPP webpage and are encouraged to contact us directly to develop a research proposal. NPP application information
  • ROSES Proposal Participation - Postdoctoral researchers have opportunities to collaborate with branch scientists on NASA Research Opportunities in Space and Earth Sciences (ROSES) proposals. These collaborations provide valuable experience in proposal development and can lead to funded research positions. Contact us for details

Collaborative Research

We actively seek partnerships with universities, research institutions, and international space agencies:

  • ROSES Solicitations - NASA Research Opportunities in Space and Earth Sciences funding calls. Current ROSES opportunities
  • Joint Research Projects - Collaborative opportunities for institutions with complementary capabilities. Inquire about collaborations
